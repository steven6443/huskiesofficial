Information we collect

We collect information in three ways: what you give us directly, what we gather automatically as you use the site, and what we receive from third parties.

Information you provide

  • Contact details. When you sign up for the Husky Owner’s Cheat Sheet or any other resource, we collect your first name and email address.
  • Messages. If you email us or otherwise contact us, we keep the contents of that correspondence and any information you choose to include.
  • Preferences. The topics or products you tell us you’re interested in (for example, tapping “Notify me” on an upcoming guide).

Information we collect automatically

  • Device and log data. IP address, browser type, device type, operating system, referring pages, and the dates and times you access the site.
  • Usage data. Pages viewed, links clicked, and how you navigate the site.
  • Campaign parameters. When you arrive from one of our posts, we may read UTM tags (such as utm_source) in the link so we know which channel sent you.
  • Cookies and similar technologies. See Cookies and tracking below.

Information from third parties

If you interact with us on a third-party platform (such as Facebook or Instagram) or reach us through their advertising or forms, we may receive limited information from that platform in accordance with its policies and your settings. Our email provider also gives us delivery and engagement data, such as whether a message was opened.

How we use your information

We use personal information to:

  • Deliver the free guides and resources you request and confirm your subscription;
  • Send you community updates, tips, new resources, and occasional announcements you’ve opted into;
  • Understand which channels and content resonate, so we can improve the site and our community;
  • Respond to your questions and provide support;
  • Maintain the security and integrity of the site and prevent fraud or abuse;
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our Terms of Service.

Where required by law (for example, in the European Economic Area and the UK), we rely on the following legal bases: your consent (which you may withdraw at any time); our legitimate interests in operating and improving our community; and compliance with legal obligations.

Email and your choices

Our email sign-up uses confirmed (double) opt-in: after you subscribe, you’ll receive an email asking you to confirm before we add you to our list. Every marketing email includes an unsubscribe link, and you can opt out at any time. You can also email us at hello@huskiesofficial.com to be removed. We may still send you essential, non-promotional messages related to a request you make.

We do not sell your personal information, and we never rent or trade your email address.

Cookies and tracking

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep the site working, remember your preferences, and understand how the site is used. Some are strictly necessary; others help us measure traffic and performance. We use Google Analytics to understand how visitors find and use the site (such as pages viewed and which guide or community link you click) — Google may set its own cookies and process this data under its own privacy policy. You can control cookies through your browser settings — blocking some may affect how the site functions, and you can opt out of Google Analytics using Google's browser add-on.

How we share information

We share personal information only in these limited circumstances:

  • Service providers. Companies that operate the site and our communications on our behalf — for example, our email marketing platform (Kit, formerly ConvertKit), website hosting, and analytics providers (Google Analytics). They may process your information only to provide services to us.
  • Legal and safety. When we believe disclosure is reasonably necessary to comply with a law, regulation, legal process, or governmental request, or to protect the rights, property, or safety of HuskiesOfficial, our community, or others.
  • Business transfers. In connection with a merger, acquisition, financing, or sale of assets, in which case information may be transferred as part of that transaction.
  • With your direction. When you ask us to share your information or make it public.

We do not sell your personal information and do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising in exchange for money.

Data retention

We keep personal information for as long as needed to provide the community and resources you’ve requested, and as required to meet our legal, accounting, or reporting obligations. When you unsubscribe or ask us to delete your data, we remove or de-identify it, except where we’re required to retain certain records.

Data security

We take reasonable administrative, technical, and organizational measures to protect your information against loss, misuse, and unauthorized access. No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, however, so we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Your privacy rights

Depending on where you live, you may have some or all of the following rights regarding your personal information:

  • Access — request a copy of the personal information we hold about you;
  • Correction — ask us to fix inaccurate or incomplete information;
  • Deletion — ask us to delete your personal information;
  • Restriction and objection — ask us to limit or stop certain processing;
  • Portability — receive your information in a portable format;
  • Withdraw consent — where we rely on consent, withdraw it at any time;
  • Opt out of “sale” or “sharing” — although we do not sell or share your information as those terms are commonly defined;
  • Non-discrimination — we won’t treat you differently for exercising your rights.

To exercise any of these rights, email us at hello@huskiesofficial.com. We may need to verify your identity before responding. If you’re in the EEA or UK and believe we’ve mishandled your data, you have the right to complain to your local data protection authority.

California privacy rights

If you’re a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the California Privacy Rights Act (CPRA), gives you the rights to know what personal information we collect and how we use it, to access and delete it, to correct inaccuracies, to opt out of the “sale” or “sharing” of your personal information, and to not be discriminated against for exercising your rights.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it for cross-context behavioral advertising, as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA — and we have not done so in the preceding 12 months. Because we don’t sell or share your personal information, we don’t offer a separate “Do Not Sell or Share My Personal Information” opt-out; there is nothing to opt out of. You can still exercise any of your rights by emailing us at hello@huskiesofficial.com.

You may use an authorized agent to submit a request on your behalf; we may ask the agent for proof of authorization and may still verify your identity directly. Under California’s “Shine the Light” law, California residents may also request information about disclosures of personal information to third parties for their direct marketing purposes — we do not disclose your personal information for third parties’ own direct marketing.

International transfers

We operate online and may process and store information in countries other than where you live, including the United States. Where required, we take steps to ensure your information receives an adequate level of protection when transferred internationally.

Children’s privacy

The site is intended for adults and is not directed to children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16 (or the minimum age required in your jurisdiction). If you believe a child has provided us with personal information, please contact us and we will delete it.

Third-party links

Our site and emails may link to third-party sites and platforms — such as Facebook, Instagram, and the products we recommend. We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those third parties, and we encourage you to review their policies.

Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we’ll revise the “Last updated” date above, and for material changes we’ll take reasonable steps to let you know. Your continued use of the site after an update means you accept the revised policy.
